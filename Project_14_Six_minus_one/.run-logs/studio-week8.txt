===== PAGE 5 =====
Prototype Testing
Split your team into two groups – A and B roughly the same size. 
• Round 1: Group A stays at your team's station to guide participants through the testing 
session. Group B leaves to test another team's prototype as participants.
• Round 2: Groups swap roles - Group B returns to guide, and Group A leaves to test another 
team's prototype.

===== PAGE 6 =====
Prototype Testing 
Step 1: Preparation (10 mins)
• Set up your prototype on a laptop
• Discuss as a team what your research question is for this testing session
• Outline the step-by-step process and instructions you will give to participants (e.g. a brief 
introduction to the project and target audience, specific tasks or scenarios to complete, and 
follow-up questions)
• Prepare any tools or materials needed  to effectively conduct the testing (e.g. a notebook 
and pen for quick notes, or a Google Form or similar tool if you prefer to collect some of the 
responses digitally)

===== PAGE 7 =====
Prototype Testing 
Step 1: First round of testing (20 mins)
If you are Group A (guiding the testing session):
•  Greet the participants and briefly introduce your project and its target audience
•  Walk participants through the tasks or scenarios you prepared in Step 1
•  Observe and record participant responses, behaviours, and any insights
•  Ask your follow-up questions 
If you are Group B (participating in another team's session):
• Engage with the tasks you are given
• Provide constructive and specific feedback
• Note any observations that could be useful when you return to your own team

===== PAGE 8 =====
Prototype Testing 
Step 2: Recap and Swap (5 mins)
All team members return to team's desk. 
Before swapping roles, take a few minutes to debrief as a team how the session went - share any 
issues, unexpected reactions, or anything that didn't go as planned (e.g. prototype bugs or broken 
flows)

===== PAGE 9 =====
Prototype Testing 
Step 3: Second round of testing (20 mins)
If you are Group B (guiding the testing session):
•  Greet the participants and briefly introduce your project and its target audience
•  Walk participants through the tasks or scenarios you prepared in Step 1
•  Observe and record participant responses, behaviours, and any insights
•  Ask your follow-up questions
